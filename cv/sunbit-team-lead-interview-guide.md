# Team Lead Interview Analysis - Sunbit

## Position Overview
**Role:** Merchant-Ops Squad Lead, Sunbit  
**Type:** Phone Interview (First Round)  
**Focus:** Leadership, Technical Architecture, Team Management

## Key Requirements Analysis

### **Must-Have Requirements (Focus Areas):**
- **2+ years leadership experience** guiding teams and making architectural decisions
- **8+ years software engineering** with focus on scalable architecture
- **Technical leadership** for merchant-facing products
- **Multidisciplinary team management** (frontend, backend, data engineers)
- **Stakeholder collaboration** with PMs and business stakeholders
- **System design** and performance optimization
- **Culture of technical excellence**

### **Highly Valued/Preferred:**
- Kotlin/Java experience
- Frontend and mobile technologies
- AWS/cloud platforms
- Spring Framework
- Relational/NoSQL databases
- Message queue technologies (Kafka, SQS, RabbitMQ)

## What to Focus On in Phone Interview

### **1. Leadership & Team Management Stories**
**Prepare examples of:**
- Leading technical teams through challenges
- Making architectural decisions that impacted business outcomes
- Balancing multiple stakeholders (PMs, business, engineering)
- Fostering culture of excellence and ownership
- Incident response and system reliability

### **2. Technical Architecture Experience**
**Be ready to discuss:**
- Scalable system design you've led
- Merchant-facing or customer-facing platforms
- Microservices/distributed systems experience
- Performance optimization initiatives
- System monitoring and incident response

### **3. Sunbit-Specific Understanding**
Research Sunbit's:
- **Merchant platform** - how they serve 25,000+ locations
- **Technical stack** (likely Java/Kotlin microservices)
- **Business model** - POS financing solutions
- **Engineering culture** - mention "Unleash your Curiosity"

## Strategic Questions to Ask

### **Team & Leadership Focus:**
1. "What's the current composition of the Merchant-Ops squad? How many frontend, backend, and data engineers?"
2. "What are the biggest technical challenges the team is facing right now?"
3. "How do you measure success for a squad lead in this role beyond just delivery?"

### **Technical & Architecture:**
4. "What's the current tech stack for merchant-facing products? Any plans for modernization?"
5. "How does the team balance technical excellence with business deadlines?"
6. "What are the key architectural decisions I'd be leading in the first 6 months?"

### **Growth & Impact:**
7. "What growth opportunities are there for this role? Could you share about previous squad leads and their career progression?"
8. "How does this team interact with other R&D squads at Sunbit?"

## Red Flags to Watch For

### **Company Culture Questions:**
- "What does 'fast-paced environment' mean in practice?" (mentioned twice in JD)
- "How does the team handle work-life balance during high-pressure periods?"
- "What's the average tenure for engineers on the team?"

### **Role Clarity:**
- "What would success look like in the first 30-60-90 days?"
- "How is the squad lead's performance measured?"
- "What are the biggest pain points the previous person in this role faced?"

## Preparation Strategy

### **Research Sunbit:**
- Check Glassdoor for engineering culture reviews
- Look up recent news/funding rounds
- Study their mobile app and merchant platform
- Understand their financing model

### **Personal Stories to Prepare:**
- **Leadership moment:** When you guided a team through technical challenge
- **Architecture decision:** Trade-off you made and why
- **Stakeholder management:** Difficult conversation with PM/business
- **Cultural impact:** How you improved team practices

### **Technical Talking Points:**
- "I've led system designs for platforms serving X customers"
- "My approach balances scalability with business needs"
- "I believe in data-driven technical decision making"

## Next Steps
After this call, you should:
1. Send thank-you email highlighting 2-3 key points discussed
2. Research answers to any questions you couldn't address
3. Prepare for potential technical follow-up

**Focus:** Show you can bridge technical leadership with business impact while building strong teams.
